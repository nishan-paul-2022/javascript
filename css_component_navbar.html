I watched Drishyam 2 (Malayalam, 2021) right after it went online. Still, I was very keen to experience the Hindi remake (2022). And it was worth it, undoubtedly. Though, overall, the original is surely better in some aspects. Performance of the shadow police couple is kind of a letdown here. Sequences that got Vijay and his family trapped were not that appealing compared to the Malayalam edition. The shocking scene revealing the true identity of the shadow police was a failed attempt to create enough shock.

But the Hindi version has something special that makes it a must-see. That is none other than Akshaye Khanna. His cold-blooded expression, body language, and appearance make him the best possible casting for this ruthless role. His screen presence was too powerful to be ignored, for sure. This guy simply owned the screen and delivered an outstanding performance. A 10/10 acting from Akshaye Khanna.

Tabu did a fabulous job as a devastated mother. I mean, you can never underestimate a mother who lost her only child, right? Her humble threat during the ritual for her dead son is just a mere indication of how far she can go to bring back justice (?).

Drishyam 2 (Hindi, 2022) is a worthy remake, I must say.
